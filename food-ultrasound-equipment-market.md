<p><p>The food ultrasound equipment market can be divided into two main types: high-frequency low-intensity and low-frequency high-intensity. High-frequency low-intensity ultrasound is primarily used for non-destructive testing, enhancing food preservation, and quality control by detecting defects without altering the food structure. Conversely, low-frequency high-intensity ultrasound is utilized for processes such as emulsification, homogenization, and extraction, effectively improving food texture and flavor while increasing efficiency in food processing. Each type serves unique applications in the food industry.</p></p>
